Breath of the Wild Review



Breath of the Wild is an open-world RPG about a protagonist named Link, who has to save the princess named Zelda. This game was released on the Nintedno Swich on March 3rd, 2017 and has become revolutionary game that has redefined and changed the open world genre. It is known for its vast open world and total freedom, which allows players to take as much time as they want to explore. There is not a linear goal or a fixed path, and players have the freedom to do any quest they like in any order. Whatever side path or quest they take, it all builds up to one goal, which is to defeat Calamity Ganon.

Characters

Link

Link is a Hylian, which is a race that has elfish features such as long, pointed ears. He has blond hair, blue eyes, and is canonically described as handsome. He is physically strong and is known for his stoic silent personality. Link does not have any dialogue in the game. Instead, he communicates through grunts, yells, and humming sounds.



Link is the main protagonist of Breath of the Wild. At the beginning of the game, he wakes up in the Shrine of Resurrection after being asleep for 1,000 years. Shortly after waking up, he hears Princess Zelda’s voice urging him to regain his strength and recover his lost memories so he can rescue her and defeat Calamity Ganon. In order to do this, Link must explore Hyrule, gear up and exploring ancient ruins to grow powerful enough to defeat the Calamity.



Before the Great Calamity 1000 years ago, Link served as Princess Zelda’s personal guard. Together with the Champions, they fought to stop Calamity Ganon but ultimately failed, and Link became severely injured. To save him and the world, Zelda placed Link inside the Shrine of Resurrection to heal, knowing he would awaken 1,000 years later to defeat Ganon. When Link wakes up at the start of the game, his memories are gone and he must journey across Hyrule to regain his strength and memories, confront Calamity Ganon, and free Zelda, who is holding him back within Hyrule Castle.

Zelda

Zelda is the deuteragonist of the story. She is a Hylian and was the princess of Hyrule 1000 years ago. She has blond hair, blue eyes, and pointy ears. She is a highly intelligent and determined person, and is burdened with the task of being the princess of Hyrule. She struggles with self-doubt because of her unability to unlock her divine powers, which has led her to study ancient text in order to help her people and find a way to unlock her powers. While she maintains a composed and resiliant personality for her kingdom, she reveals moments of vulnerability, doubt, and frustration over being unable to unlock her divine powers.

Before the Great Calamity, she has fought with the Champions and Link in order to stop Calamity Ganon. However, it was unsuccessful and she had to place Link in the Shrine of Resurrection and seal Calamity Ganon away for 1000 years. After Link wakes up 1000 years later, she was the one that told Link to regain his memory and power in order to slay Calamity Ganon and free her. She sacrified her body in order to seal Calamity Ganon for 1000 years in hopes that Link will recover and save Hyrule.

At the ene of Breath of the Wild, Link goes into Castle Hyrule to slay Calamity Ganon and free Zelda. He has freed the Divine Beasts which helps weaken Calamity Ganon and has gained the powers of the champions. Calamity has two forms that Link has to defeat, and with the help of the Bow of Light, Link defeats Ganon and saves Hyrule. If you collected all of Link's missing memory, you would unlock the true ending with a bonus cutscene of Link and Zelda after the battle.
